<Callout type="info" title="Quick Guide">
- **Subterra Crafting** is strongest when you save rare mats, craft for survival first, and upgrade efficiency second.
- **Potions, keys, and explosives** usually beat luxury gear if you want smoother runs and faster returns.
- **Gem materials** like Topaz, Amethyst, Ruby, Sapphire, and Diamond are better saved than spent early.
- **Weapon crafting** matters most once deeper layers start punishing weak damage and slow clear speed.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Subterra Crafting Priorities">

Subterra Crafting works best when every recipe has a job. If an item does not improve your next mining run, survival window, or stash efficiency, it can usually wait. That mindset keeps your materials flexible instead of locked into a weak upgrade path.

<Callout type="tip" title="Core Rule">
Craft for the next expedition, not for the inventory screen. A good recipe should either save time, reduce deaths, or unlock better loot.
</Callout>

| Priority | Craft First | Why It Matters | Hold Off On |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Small Health Potion | Restores HP and keeps long runs alive | Cosmetic or low-impact spending |
| **2** | Small Sticky Bomb / Sticky Dynamite | Helps clear blocks and open routes | Random crafting with no route plan |
| **3** | Iron Key / Gold Key | Opens better chest rewards | Burning rare mats on side recipes |
| **4** | Basic ingots and block conversion | Supports multiple future recipes | Spending raw ore too early |
| **5** | Weapons for tougher layers | Stabilizes combat before deeper dives | Chasing perfect damage too soon |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Best Recipes and Materials to Track">

The safest approach is to separate materials into three buckets: consume, save, and convert. That makes crafting decisions faster when your backpack is full and enemies are closing in.

<Callout type="warning" title="Do Not Overcommit">
Rare gems and deep-tier ores are easy to waste on low-impact recipes. If a material appears in multiple future upgrades, keep it in reserve.
</Callout>

| Recipe | Ingredients | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Small Health Potion** | Water Flask, Roots, Life Shard | Restores **25 HP** | Early survival and deeper cave runs |
| **Small Sticky Bomb** | Slime Chunk, Small Bomb | Sticky explosive for controlled clearing | Opening ore pockets and tight tunnels |
| **Sticky Dynamite** | Slime Chunk, Dynamite | Stronger sticky blast | Mid-game route clearing |
| **Sticky Dynamites** | Slime Chunk, Dynamites | Wider sticky explosive support | Bigger excavation jobs |
| **Sticky C4** | Slime Chunk, C4 | High-radius sticky explosive | Late-game block clearing |
| **Stone** | 4x Rock | Converts basic rock into Stone | Simple block crafting utility |
| **Iron Key** | Recipe progression item | Opens Iron Chests | Mid-value chest routes |
| **Gold Key** | Recipe progression item | Opens Gold Chests | High-value loot runs |

| Material | Save or Spend | Main Uses | Practical Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coal** | Save early | Pickaxe and backpack recipes, crafting routes | Spend only when the next tier is ready |
| **Copper / Tin** | Save for progression | Early ingots and upgrade chains | Do not break your material flow with side crafts |
| **Iron / Silver / Gold** | Save for major tiers | Midgame tools, storage, and crafting steps | These are strong gate materials |
| **Topaz / Amethyst** | Save | Backpack and recipe gates | Better kept than sold too early |
| **Emerald / Ruby / Sapphire / Diamond** | Save aggressively | High-tier progression and late recipes | Treat them as future value, not spare currency |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Crafting Loadouts and Upgrade Timing">

Crafting is easier when your build matches your route. A miner focused on long tunnels needs different recipes than a player pushing deeper layers or clearing monsters. The goal is not the most expensive item; it is the item that keeps the run moving.

<Callout type="tip" title="Build Around the Run">
Choose one weapon, one utility layer, and one resource plan. That avoids spread-thin crafting and keeps your inventory under control.
</Callout>

| Playstyle | Weapon Focus | Useful Cards | Crafting Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Deep Ore Farmer** | Reliable melee damage | Lucky, Explosive Touch, Blazing Pickaxe, Bigger Backpack | Maximize ore output and tunnel speed |
| **Safe Explorer** | Balanced melee or ranged | Healthy, Double Jump, Walk Speed, Extra Oxygen | Survive longer and travel farther |
| **Smeltery Runner** | Stable, low-risk weapon | Blazing Pickaxe, Larger Furnace, Lighter Pickaxe, Pouch | Reduce downtime between mining and processing |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Sort Your Backpack Before Crafting">
    Check which materials are common, which are rare, and which appear in more than one recipe. Move rare gems into the keep pile immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Craft Survival Tools First">
    Make the recipes that recover health, open chests, or clear blocks faster. These usually have the best short-term return.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Lock In Your Route">
    Decide whether the next session is for ore farming, chest hunting, or combat. That choice tells you whether to spend on explosives, keys, or weapons.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Upgrade Only When the Bottleneck Is Clear">
    If you are dying, craft defense. If you are full too early, improve storage. If you are stuck in hard layers, move into stronger weapons.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Timing Check">
A recipe is usually worth it when it removes a repeat problem. If it does not fix a real bottleneck, keep your materials.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="What to Keep in Your Inventory">

Material discipline is the difference between smooth progression and wasted farming time. Keep your most flexible resources for recipes that unlock repeated value, not single-use comfort items.

<Callout type="warning" title="Inventory Rule">
Do not treat rare materials like extra gold. If a gem or ingot can unlock a stronger recipe later, it has more value than a quick sale.
</Callout>

| **Combat resources** | Slime Chunk, potion ingredients, explosives | When the route benefits from speed or safety | Directly affects survival |

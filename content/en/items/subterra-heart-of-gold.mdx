<Section icon={Target} title="Upgrade Order for Faster Profit">

The best upgrade order is the one that removes bottlenecks first. In Subterra, that usually means mining speed, then carry space, then utility. If your pickaxe is slow, more backpack space only gives you a longer wait. If your backpack is tiny, stronger mining just sends you home earlier.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Claim your early boosts">
    Redeem active codes before you commit to long mining loops. Chrono Shards, TNT, EXP, and potions create breathing room for the first upgrades.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Upgrade the pickaxe first">
    A stronger pickaxe improves mining speed and block-breaking power, which makes every route faster and more flexible.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Upgrade the backpack second">
    Move into backpack upgrades once your trips start ending because of inventory space, not because of mining speed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Lock in deeper-layer spending">
    Hold rare gems and materials for planned recipes, then move into deeper layers only when your run can stay profitable.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Priority | Spend On | Why It Helps | Skip If |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pickaxe | Faster block break and better depth access | Mining already feels instant |
| 2 | Backpack | Longer runs and fewer surface trips | You still return with half-empty space |
| 3 | Smelting stock | Prevents recipe delays | You are missing core ores |
| 4 | Ability Cards | Improves route efficiency | Your basic mining loop is not stable yet |

<Callout type="info" title="Upgrade Rule">
If you are debating between damage, storage, and speed, choose speed first. Faster mining solves more problems than a slightly better combat stat in the early game.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Loadout and Checklist for Profit Runs">

A good profit run is built around consistency. You want enough mobility to exit bad fights, enough storage to keep valuable ore, and enough utility to stretch each trip. That approach keeps the route focused on profit instead of panic.

<Callout type="success" title="Loadout Goal">
Build around one reliable weapon, one mobility card, one storage card, and one survival card. That mix keeps the run efficient without overcommitting to any single situation.
</Callout>

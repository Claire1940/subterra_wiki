<Section icon={Target} title="Best Farming Route and Spawn Priority">

Once you enter a farming zone, your first job is to remove randomness. The best route is the one you can repeat without panic, because elite-core farming gets much better when you stop overextending.

<Callout type="tip" title="Route Rule">
Fight in areas with room to move. If a tunnel gives you no space to dodge, pull enemies back into a wider lane before you commit.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Set Your Exit Point">
    Choose a safe return path before the fight starts. If your inventory fills or your health drops, you should already know where to leave.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Clear Weak Mobs First">
    Remove small enemies and nuisance targets before you focus on elite enemies. Swarms create mistakes, and mistakes cost runs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Pull Elites Into Open Space">
    Let elite enemies come to you in a wider area. Open ground makes dodging easier and reduces the chance of getting trapped.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reset After a Clean Loop">
    Leave while your run still feels controlled. A clean exit with a full bag is better than a risky push that ends the session early.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Priority | What to Target | Why It Matters | What to Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Elite enemies | Best chance at core-focused farming | Chasing them through cramped dead ends |
| 2 | Ranged threats | They punish bad movement | Ignoring them while you tunnel vision |
| 3 | Swarm packs | They block routes and drain healing | Focusing them after your health is already low |
| 4 | Boss guards | High pressure, high risk | Fighting them without an exit plan |

| Run Trigger | Leave Now If... | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Health check | Healing is nearly gone | A bad trade can snowball fast |
| Inventory check | Your bag is close to full | You want the run to stay efficient |
| Ammo or stamina check | Your attack rhythm falls apart | Sloppy tempo leads to missed windows |
| Position check | You lose your escape lane | Reposition before you get boxed in |

<Callout type="warning" title="Do Not Greed">
If a route starts forcing risky fights, reset it. Farming only works when the next run starts in a better state than the last one ended.
</Callout>

A few card choices stand out for this kind of farming. **Lucky** helps if the route also feeds ore value. **Explosive Touch** is excellent when enemies cluster near breakable terrain. **Blazing Pickaxe** saves time if you are converting the same trip into both combat and mining value. **Healthy** and **Walk Speed** are the safest early picks when you are still learning enemy timing.

| Tool Slot | Smart Choice | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|

| Mobility | Double Jump or Walk Speed | Makes repositioning much easier |
| Economy | Bigger Backpack | Cuts down on return trips |
| Utility | Extra Oxygen | Extends deep-route uptime |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Glass Cannon Traps">

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Farming Checklist and Quick Comparison">

A good farming plan is easy to repeat. Before each session, confirm your route, your escape path, and the one or two upgrades that matter most right now.

| Question | Good Answer | Bad Answer |
|---|---|---|
| Can I leave safely? | Yes, the exit path is clear | No, I have to improvise |
| Can I survive a mistake? | Yes, I have sustain or mobility | No, one hit can end the run |
| Is my bag helping me? | Yes, I can hold more value | No, I return too often |
| Am I killing quickly enough? | Yes, fights end before control breaks | No, I stay in danger too long |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Confirm Your Goal">
    Decide whether the session is for drops, money, or practice. A focused goal prevents wasted movement.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Match Your Loadout">
    Pick cards and gear that solve the route's biggest weakness, not the one you wish you had.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Run a Short Test Loop">
    Do one controlled run first. If it feels unstable, adjust before you commit a long session.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Lock In the Best Pattern">
    Repeat the route that gives the best balance of safety, speed, and reward density.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Best Loadout for Frozen Chest Runs">

The best frozen-chest loadout is the one that keeps your run stable long enough to cash out. A chest in a frozen layer is only valuable if you can reach it, open it, and survive the trip back. Build around movement, survivability, and one reliable weapon, then spend your remaining slots on mining speed or carry space.

<Callout type="tip" title="Loadout Rule">
If you cannot survive a mistake, your build is too greedy. Frozen routes punish players who optimize only for damage or only for mining speed.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Route to the Frozen Chest">

Use a simple route instead of improvising underground. The cleanest method is to prep in the lobby, enter the deeper layer path, secure the area, and then open the chest only when your exit is clear. This is the kind of loop that keeps frozen runs profitable instead of chaotic.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prep in the surface lobby">
    Empty unnecessary items, keep your core tools, and make sure your backpack has room for a worthwhile chest run.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Enter the deep-layer path">
    Aim for the frozen corridor through Darkstone or straight into Permafrost if your build is strong enough.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Clear a safe pocket first">
    Break blocks around the chest, remove enemy pressure, and make space so you can fight without getting trapped.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Open the chest and extract immediately">
    Loot fast, grab any high-value materials, and leave before the room fills with enemies or bad terrain.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Checkpoint | What to do | Why it helps |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lobby prep | Clear inventory and confirm tools | Reduces wasted time underground |
| Deep entrance | Move toward the frozen route | Keeps your trip focused |
| Safe pocket | Remove block clutter and enemies | Prevents panic deaths |
| Chest opening | Loot, then rotate out | Preserves profit and momentum |

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Route Mistake">
Players often mine directly into the chest room without creating an exit lane. That works until the first enemy or explosion blocks the way out.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="What to Keep, Sell, or Spend First">

Frozen chest runs become much better when the loot has a job. Anything that supports upgrades, survival, or deeper routing should stay in your inventory. Everything else should move toward selling, smelting, or crafting as soon as possible. That keeps the next run stronger than the last one.

<Callout type="success" title="Profit Rule">
Keep items that unlock future progress. Sell only the extras that do not move your pickaxe, backpack, cards, or combat setup forward.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Frozen Chest Mistakes and FAQ">

This section is about correcting bad habits before they cost a run. Frozen areas punish hesitation, bad spacing, and greedy looting. If you can avoid those traps, the chest becomes a reliable part of your progression path instead of a one-off win.

<Callout type="info" title="Execution Tip">
Your goal is not just opening the chest. Your goal is opening it and leaving with enough resources to make the next frozen run easier.
</Callout>

| Mistake | Result | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- |

| Spending rare gems too early | Slower upgrades later | Save gems for tier requirements |
| Ignoring inventory space | Forced drop-offs | Upgrade backpack or leave faster |
| Looting before securing the room | Death during pickup | Clear enemies first |

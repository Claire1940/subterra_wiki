<Section icon={Map} title="Understanding Subterra Ability Cards Mechanics">

Subterra Ability Cards represent the core progression system that sits alongside your pickaxe and backpack upgrades. While tools provide the raw stats needed to break blocks, cards provide the utility and specialized bonuses required to survive the increasingly hostile layers like Darkstone and Permafrost. These cards are managed through your inventory and can be swapped at the surface hub to prepare for specific mining routes or boss encounters.

The system relies on "Slots," which limit how many cards you can have active at once. Early in the game, your slot capacity is low, forcing difficult choices between survival and profit. As you progress and complete specific milestones, you unlock more slots, allowing for complex "Builds" that can automate smelting, double your ore yields, or provide the mobility needed to navigate vertical ruins and floating islands.

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Obtain and Upgrade Cards">

Acquiring new **Subterra Ability Cards** requires a steady supply of Chrono Shards. These shards are not found like regular ores; instead, they are rewarded for your milestones as a spelunker. To spend them, you must return to the Overworld and interact with the Chrono Crystal located near the Plaza.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Collect Chrono Shards">
    Complete achievements such as "Worldbreaker" (10,000 blocks) or "Chest King" (80 chests) to earn bulk amounts of shards. You can also use active codes like "1YEAR" to get an immediate 1,400 shard boost.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Visit the Chrono Crystal">
    Navigate to the surface hub and find the glowing purple crystal. This is the only location where you can "Summon" new cards into your inventory.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Summon and Manage">
    Spend your shards to receive a random card. Check the rarity rates before pulling; during certain events, specific card types may have boosted drop rates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Equip and Stack">
    Open your inventory (E) and drag the cards into your active slots. Remember to check the "Card Limit" on each card to ensure you aren't wasting a slot on a non-stackable effect.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Inventory Management">
Ability Cards take up space in your general inventory if not equipped. If your backpack is full of ores, you might find yourself unable to pick up new card drops from chests. Always keep 2-3 slots open for rare loot.
</Callout>

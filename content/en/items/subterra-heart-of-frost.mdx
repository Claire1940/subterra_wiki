<Section icon={Target} title="Best Frost Route and Layer Priority">

The best route is not the deepest one on paper. It is the one that turns every trip into progress. Start where enemies are manageable, then move deeper only when your inventory, tools, and recovery are ready.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Stabilize at the Surface">
    Empty your inventory, buy what you actually need, and decide whether the run is for ore, quests, or boss prep.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Push Through Darkstone First">
    Use Darkstone as your transition layer. Farm what you can, but do not linger if your clear speed slows down.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Farm Permafrost Efficiently">
    Focus on the highest-value ore pockets, nearby lootables, and any route that can be cleared without constant backtracking.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Enter Schwarzfrost Only With Purpose">
    Go deeper when you are ready for stronger enemies, boss-adjacent loot, or advanced resource farming.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Leave Before the Run Turns Negative">
    Exit when healing runs low, inventory fills, or enemy pressure starts costing more time than the trip earns.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Route Step | What to Farm | What to Avoid | Exit Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Darkstone setup | Cobalt, Nocturnite, black-ice routes | Long fights with skeletons | Damage starts slowing down |
| Permafrost farm | Platinum, Chromium, Moonstone, Charged Ice | Wandering without a target | Bag is nearly full |
| Schwarzfrost push | Endgame loot, boss prep resources | Greedy exploration loops | Health or oxygen becomes tight |

<Callout type="warning" title="Route Discipline">

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Upgrades, Materials, and What to Save">

Frost progression gets expensive fast, so resource discipline matters. The upgrade tables show a clear pattern: pickaxe first when mining slows, backpack second when trips end too early, and rare gems only when a recipe actually asks for them.

<Callout type="info" title="Save These Materials">
Keep rare gems, ingots, and key crafting items in reserve. Spending them too early can delay both upgrade tiers and deep-layer route stability.
</Callout>

| Resource | Best Use | Do Not Waste It On | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Coal | Early and mid upgrade chains | Random side crafts | Needed in multiple routes |
| Copper / Tin | Early progression and ingots | Low-value filler items | Helps bridge into stronger tiers |
| Iron / Silver / Gold | Midgame crafting and upgrades | Nonessential rerolls | Important for stable progression |
| Platinum / Chromium | Advanced crafting and storage | Quick disposable crafts | Strong late-route value |
| Citrine / Topaz / Amethyst / Emerald / Ruby / Sapphire / Diamond | Higher-tier recipes | Blind spending | Save for actual upgrade requirements |

| Priority | Upgrade Target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pickaxe | Faster mining and better block handling |
| 2 | Backpack | Longer routes and fewer return trips |
| 3 | Cards | Better efficiency for your preferred playstyle |
| 4 | Consumables | Emergency safety in deep frost runs |

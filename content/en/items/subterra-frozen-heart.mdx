<Section icon={Target} title="Best Farming Route for Frozen Heart">

If your goal is to farm Frozen Heart efficiently, build a repeatable route before you worry about optimization. The cleanest runs usually start with preparation, move through the cold zone, and end the moment the bag gets awkward.

<Callout type="tip" title="Route Mindset">
Don’t chase every side pocket. A tight route with predictable returns usually produces better results than a wide, slow crawl.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prepare Before You Leave">
    Empty unnecessary items, keep healing options ready, and make sure your inventory has space for valuable drops.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Enter the Coldest Layer You Can Handle">
    Move only as deep as your gear can support. If the zone slows you down too much, the route is too ambitious.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Mine in a Loop, Not a Line">
    Clear a repeatable path so you can backtrack quickly. Clean loops reduce dead ends and wasted travel time.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reset Before the Run Turns Sloppy">
    Leave while the route is still efficient. A smart reset often protects more progress than one extra dangerous room.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Step | Action | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Prep | Clear inventory, ready recovery items | Less downtime and fewer forced exits |
| Enter | Push to your highest stable cold zone | Better chance of meaningful farming |
| Loop | Follow a predictable path | Faster returns and safer navigation |
| Reset | Leave before risk spikes | Preserves gains and reduces losses |

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Route Mistake">
The biggest mistake is overextending for one more room. If your path stops feeling clean, the run is already losing value.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Uses, Priorities, and Common Mistakes">

Once you secure Frozen Heart, the next question is how to spend that progress well. In most cases, rare late-game targets should support your route, not distract from it.

<Callout type="success" title="Resource Discipline">
Hold onto rare materials until they clearly improve your next farming loop. Spending too early can slow down later progression.
</Callout>

| Use Case | Why It Matters | Priority |
|---|---|---:|
| Late-game crafting | Supports stronger progression | High |
| Route upgrades | Improves future farming runs | High |
| Trading or saving | Keeps rare value flexible | Medium |
| Side projects | Useful, but not urgent | Low |

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Rushing deeper too early | Leads to wasted time and deaths | Farm the highest stable zone instead |
| Ignoring inventory pressure | Forces bad exits or lost value | Reset earlier and often |
| Building for damage only | Makes the route harder to sustain | Add mobility and recovery first |
| Spreading effort across too many paths | Lowers consistency | Keep one primary loop |

<Callout type="info" title="Best Priority Order">

</Callout>

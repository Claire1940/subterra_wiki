<Section icon={Shield} title="Upgrade Order Before You Push Deeper">

The cleanest progression in Subterra is still the boring one: improve mining power first, then increase inventory space, then layer in cards that solve your current problem. That order keeps your runs profitable and reduces the number of emergency returns to the surface.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Finish the tutorial and unlock the lobby loop">
    Learn the spawn area, locate the Codes NPC, and make the lobby your reset point before every serious mining trip.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Upgrade your pickaxe before anything flashy">
    Pickaxe tiers improve both mining power and speed, which makes every later route more efficient.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Add backpack space when trips end too early">
    Backpack upgrades are best when your run stops because of storage, not because the cave is empty.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Spend Chrono Shards on useful cards">
    Prioritize Lucky, Bigger Backpack, Walk Speed, Healthy, or Blazing Pickaxe depending on what is slowing you down.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Move to deeper layers only after your kit feels stable">
    Darkstone and Permafrost punish weak gear, so enter them with a plan instead of hoping raw damage carries the run.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Priority | What to Upgrade | Why It Comes First |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pickaxe | Faster mining and better block handling |
| 2 | Backpack | Longer routes and fewer return trips |
| 3 | Ability Cards | Direct utility for mining or survival |
| 4 | Gems | Save for required recipes |
| 5 | Side Crafts | Only after your core loop is stable |

<Section icon={Map} title="Farming Route, Layers, and Resources">

A Fear Ripper-style route should be built around the safest resource ladder, not the deepest possible drop. Early ores fund upgrades, mid-layer gems fund recipes, and deep-layer materials fund the late-game push. The layer table below gives you a simple route that stays aligned with the current Subterra progression map.

| Layer | Key Targets | Threat Level | Best Time to Enter |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dirt Layer | Coal, Copper, Tin | Low | After the tutorial |
| Stone Layer | Iron, Silver, Gold, gems | Medium | Once basic combat is stable |
| Darkstone Layer | Cobalt, Nocturnite, Black Ice | High | When mobility and damage improve |
| Permafrost Layer | Platinum, Chromium, Moonstone, Titanium | Very High | When you can sustain longer runs |
| Schwarzfrost Layer | Charged Ice, Eiskron | Endgame | When you are ready for boss routes |

| Resource | Keep or Spend | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Rock | Spend early | Core pickaxe and quest material |
| Coal | Keep some | Needed in multiple upgrades |
| Raw Copper | Keep | Appears in early gear progression |
| Tin Ingot | Keep | Useful in mid-tier recipes |
| Iron Ingot | Keep | Core progression metal |
| Gold | Spend carefully | Common, but still needed often |
| Citrine and above | Save | Usually shows up in important recipes |
| Chrono Shards | Spend on cards | Strongest long-term utility currency |

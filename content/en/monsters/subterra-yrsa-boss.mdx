<Section icon={Swords} title="Best Loadout for the Fight">

Your loadout should solve three problems: damage, movement, and recovery. The highest-value setup is the one that keeps you alive long enough to keep swinging.

<Callout type="tip" title="Loadout Rule">
Build for the full route first, then for the boss itself. A perfect weapon cannot save a run if you arrive drained, slow, or trapped.
</Callout>

| Slot | Best Pick | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Weapon | Splitblade | Highest listed late-game pressure option for direct melee damage |
| Alternate Weapon | Knight’s Greatsword | Strong fallback if you prefer a safer rhythm |
| Card 1 | Healthy | Raises your buffer against sudden mistakes |
| Card 2 | Walk Speed | Helps with spacing and recovery |
| Card 3 | Double Jump | Improves repositioning in vertical terrain |
| Card 4 | Extra Oxygen | Extends deep-layer travel and reduces route pressure |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Pick a weapon you can use without hesitation">
    Choose a weapon that feels stable in real combat. If you miss too many swings, raw damage numbers matter less than consistency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Add movement before greed">
    Slot Walk Speed and Double Jump early. If you can disengage cleanly, you can survive longer and attack more often.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Add health support last">

  </Step>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Beat Yrsa Without Wasting Supplies">

Yrsa punishes panic more than impatience. The most effective plan is simple: keep your distance when the arena gets cramped, then re-engage only when your movement is safe.

<Callout type="info" title="Fight Logic">
You are not trying to race the boss bar. You are trying to keep your own bar from collapsing while you chip the boss down.
</Callout>

| Situation | Best Response | Mistake to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Open space | Keep pressure and maintain spacing | Running straight into repeated hits |
| Tight space | Reposition first, attack second | Swinging while boxed in |
| Low health | Break contact and recover | Greedy trading |
| Lost rhythm | Reset your angle and tempo | Spamming inputs without a plan |

| Threat Type | What It Usually Means | Safer Answer |
|---|---|---|
| Heavy hit window | You are too close for too long | Step out, then re-enter |
| Positioning mistake | Arena control is slipping | Use movement cards early |
| Resource drain | The run is lasting too long | Back off and recover |
| Overcommitment | You are forcing damage | Shorten your attack bursts |

Use this simple priority list during the fight:

- **First**, keep yourself mobile.
- **Second**, keep the boss in view.
- **Third**, only extend combos when you know you can leave safely.
- **Fourth**, save healing for real danger, not minor chip damage.

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Error">
Most failed deep-layer runs do not end because the player had zero damage. They end because the player stayed in one place too long.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Route, Checkpoints, and Pre-Fight Checklist">

Your route matters because the boss is only one part of the run. If you arrive to Yrsa’s Ship with a weak bag, empty supplies, or no backup plan, the encounter becomes much more expensive than it should be.

<Callout type="tip" title="Route Advice">
Treat the route like part of the fight. Every checkpoint should reduce risk, not create more of it.
</Callout>

| Route Stage | Goal | What to Have Ready |
|---|---|---|
| Surface Lobby | Restock and organize | Weapon, healing, cards |
| Darkstone Layer | Confirm survivability | Movement and damage support |
| Permafrost Layer | Stabilize the final approach | Oxygen and recovery |
| Yrsa’s Ship | Start the boss at full control | No clutter, no panic |

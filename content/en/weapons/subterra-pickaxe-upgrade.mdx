<Section icon={Map} title="Subterra Pickaxe Upgrade System Overview">

In the 2.5D Roblox adventure Subterra, upgrading your tools is the single most important factor for deep-layer survival. Your pickaxe determines both your mining power and your swing speed. Higher-tier blocks in deeper layers require higher mining power to break efficiently, meaning a weak starter tool will severely limit your exploration.

To execute a **subterra pickaxe upgrade**, you must gather specific blocks, raw ores, and smelted ingots, then bring them along with Gold to the Blacksmith NPC at the Overworld surface hub. Pinning your active recipes at the Blacksmith helps you track materials during active runs.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Tool Progression Route">

To progress efficiently without hitting a wall where blocks take too long to break, follow this structured upgrade path.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure the Dirt Layer Basics">
    Start by mining Rock and Coal in the Dirt Layer. Accumulate enough materials to leap directly from Tier 1 to Tier 3 quickly, bypassing the weak Tier 2 phase if possible.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish Copper and Tin Smelting">
    Descend to the Stone Layer and focus on mining Raw Copper and Raw Tin. Bring these to the Overworld Smeltery to craft Copper Ingots. This unlocks Tier 5 and Tier 6 upgrades, which provide a massive 15% boost to mining speed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Collect Citrine and Iron">
    Once you reach Tier 9, you will need Citrine Crystals and Raw Iron. Search for Gem Geodes in the Stone Layer and mine deeper (Y -100 and below) to gather Raw Iron.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finalize the Early-Mid Game Checkpoint">
    Smelt your Raw Iron into Iron Ingots to craft the Tier 13 and Tier 14 pickaxes. This positions your character perfectly to break Darkstone blocks and transition into late-game areas.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Progression Synergy">
Pair your pickaxe upgrades with backpack upgrades. A high-tier pickaxe is useless if your inventory fills up within two minutes of digging.
</Callout>

</Section>

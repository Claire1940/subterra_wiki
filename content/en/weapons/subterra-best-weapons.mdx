<Section icon={Target} title="How to Choose the Right Weapon">

Choosing the right weapon in Subterra is less about fantasy and more about route control. A good weapon should match how you move, how deep you mine, and how often you expect to fight while carrying valuable ore.

<Callout type="warning" title="Do Not Chase Damage Alone">
A weapon that looks strong on paper can still slow you down if you cannot stay alive, keep distance, or finish runs with enough inventory space to profit.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Decide Your Main Job">
    If you spend most of your time mining, prioritize consistent clearing. If you fight often, prioritize burst damage and safer positioning.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Pick Your Range">
    Greatswords are best for close-range control. Bows and longbows are better when you want to hold space in tight layers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Check Your Materials">
    Save upgrade materials before crafting. A strong weapon you cannot afford to maintain is less useful than a steady mid-tier option.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Match Cards to the Weapon">
    Use Lucky for farming, Blazing Pickaxe for smelting efficiency, and mobility cards when your route gets deeper or more dangerous.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Weapon Type | Strength | Weakness | Best When |
|---|---|---|---|
| Greatsword | High burst, easy pressure | Shorter safety window | You can control melee spacing |
| Bow | Safer ranged hits | Lower close-range control | Enemies force you to kite |
| Longbow | Better reach and spacing | Slower setup | Open tunnels and layered caves |
| Starter Sword | Cheap early fallback | Falls off fast | First hours and tutorial stage |

If you want a practical rule, use melee for confidence and bows for survival. Greatswords are the better default, but ranged weapons become more valuable when a run is already carrying rare ore and you cannot afford a wipe.

</Section>

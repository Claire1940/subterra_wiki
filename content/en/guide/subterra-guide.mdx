<Section icon={Target} title="First 30 Minutes: Set Up a Clean Mining Route">

Your first half hour should feel disciplined, not random. Complete the opening flow, learn the controls, and leave each run with a clear goal. If you always know what material you are hunting next, progression becomes much smoother.

<Callout type="tip" title="Route Rule">
If your backpack fills before your target list is done, return to the surface, sell, and upgrade before going back down.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Upgrades: Pickaxe, Backpack, and Card Priority">

Early Subterra progress is mostly about spending the right materials at the right time. Pickaxe upgrades improve mining power and speed, while backpack upgrades prevent short trips from killing momentum. Ability Cards then push your route efficiency even further.

<Callout type="warning" title="Spend Materials with Discipline">
Do not burn rare gems or midgame ingots on side upgrades if they are listed in a later recipe. A delayed upgrade is usually better than a dead-end purchase.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Layers, Ores, and Risk by Depth">

The safest way to move deeper is to treat each layer as a separate job. Know the ore band, know the threat level, and know what you are there to take home. That approach keeps the run focused and protects your profit margin.

<Callout type="success" title="Progression Logic">
Move down only when the current layer stops paying for itself. If a lower zone increases deaths more than rewards, you are there too early.
</Callout>

| Layer | Depth | Key Ores | Main Threat | Best Objective |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Overworld | Surface hub | None | Minimal | Buy, sell, craft, and prep |
| Dirt Layer | Surface to around Y -35 | Coal, Copper, Tin | Slime, Zombie | Build your first material stock |
| Stone Layer | Starts around Y -35 | Iron, Silver, Gold, gems | Slime, Zombie, Bomb Skeleton | Farm the first real progression ores |
| Darkstone Layer | Starts around Y -435 | Cobalt, Nocturnite, Black Ice | Darkstone Skeleton | Push into deeper value routes |
| Permafrost Layer | Starts around Y -723 | Chromium, Moonstone, Sunstone, Titanium | Ice Slime, Ice Elemental | Farm advanced ores and crystals |
| Schwarzfrost Layer | Deep frozen endgame | Charged Ice, Eiskron, Nocturnite | High-end enemies and boss pressure | Prepare for boss routes and top-tier loot |

| Resource Type | Best Use | Keep or Sell |
|---|---|---|
| Common ores | Early upgrades and currency | Sell some, keep some |
| Gem drops | Recipe and tier progression | Usually keep |
| Deep crystals | Late crafting and strong recipes | Keep |
| Loot from chests | Keys, scrolls, and consumables | Keep until reviewed |

The key habit is simple: mine a layer for its value, not just its depth. If a zone gives better materials but slows your route too much, return later with stronger gear.

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Best Loadouts and Crafting Rules">

A good loadout in Subterra is not only about damage. It is about how long you can stay underground, how fast you can clear blocks, and how safely you can leave with profit.

<Callout type="tip" title="Loadout Rule">
One strong weapon plus a focused card set usually beats a messy build with random bonuses.

| Crafting Priority | Good Use | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Small Health Potion | Early survival | Great when deeper runs start to sting |
| Sticky explosives | Controlled clearing | Best for dense blocks and ore pockets |

| Weapons | Enemy control | Craft before hostile layers punish you |

Build around the route you actually run. If you mostly mine, keep ore and carry bonuses high. If you keep dying in deeper caves, shift slots toward mobility and health before chasing more damage.

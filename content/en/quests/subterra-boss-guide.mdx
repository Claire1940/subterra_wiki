<Section icon={Target} title="A Safe Fight Loop for Deep-Layer Battles">

Boss fights become much easier when you follow the same rhythm every time. The goal is not to overextend; it is to make each exchange predictable. Treat the battle like a route: enter with a plan, take your window, then leave before the fight starts forcing bad decisions.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Set the loadout before you enter">
    Equip one strong weapon, one survivability card, and at least one mobility option. Top off healing before you leave the lobby or smeltery area.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Open with safe positioning">
    Move into the fight carefully and keep your camera and movement clear. If the route is cramped, do not rush the first trade.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Trade, then reset">
    Hit during safe windows, then back off. Use F to block or parry when the boss closes space, and keep an escape lane open.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Leave before the run turns negative">
    If your health, oxygen, or inventory pressure starts working against you, disengage. Boss progression is easier when you preserve resources for the next attempt.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Fight Habit | What It Prevents | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-fight healing** | Dying to the first bad trade | Cleaner opening |
| **Short attack windows** | Overcommitting in melee range | Safer sustain |
| **Escape route planning** | Getting trapped in cave corners | Better survival |
| **Early retreat** | Turning one mistake into a full wipe | Lower resource loss |

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Mistake">
Many players lose boss runs because they stay for one extra hit. That extra swing often costs more than it gains.
</Callout>

</Section>

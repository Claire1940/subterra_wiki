<Section icon={Map} title="Best Quest Route and Completion Flow">

A good quest route saves more time than a better-looking reward on paper. The fastest Subterra quests path is usually the one that keeps you on one layer for as long as possible, especially when the quest target matches the ore you already need for upgrades.

<Callout type="warning" title="Do Not Over-Route">
Do not jump into gem quests too early. If your pickaxe, backpack, or layer access is not ready, you will lose more time traveling than you gain from the reward.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Pick the right quest hub">
    Start with the hub that matches your current depth. Use starter tasks for early mining, then move into Plaza and Cave Village objectives once your route expands.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Stack the quest with your mining route">
    Mine the blocks and ores you already need for progression while you clear the objective. That keeps every swing productive.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Return only when the loop is efficient">
    Turn in the quest when your bag is nearly full, the objective is done, or the route stops producing good ore value.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Upgrade before the next run">
    Spend your Gold on the next practical tool or storage upgrade before accepting a harder objective.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Move up only when the reward fits the layer">
    If the reward no longer matches the time spent, switch to a better quest tier or a deeper mining route.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Session Length | Best Quest Focus | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| 15 minutes | First Step, Stone Breaker | Grab a quick win and log out with progress |
| 30 minutes | Delver, Topaz Seeker | Mine while building toward one upgrade |
| 60 minutes | Shiny Hoarder, Deep Delver | Commit to a longer run with a full inventory plan |

The best completion flow is still simple: accept one objective, mine with purpose, sell or craft only when necessary, and reset with a better route. That is how questing stays profitable instead of becoming a detour.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Reward Planning and Efficient Progression">

Quest rewards in Subterra are most useful when they feed the next step of your build. Gold should usually become upgrade speed, EXP should help you keep pacing up, and Chrono Shards should support broader progression systems like Ability Cards.

<Callout type="info" title="Reward Priority">
Treat Gold as your immediate upgrade fuel, EXP as your long-term pace setter, and Chrono Shards as the currency that keeps your build flexible.
</Callout>

| Reward Type | Main Source | Best Use | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gold | Quest turn-ins, general play | Pickaxe, backpack, and crafting progression | High |
| EXP | Quests, combat, exploration | Leveling and overall account growth | High |
| Chrono Shards | Achievements, some codes | Ability Card progression and build tuning | Medium |
| Materials | Mining during quest runs | Crafting, recipes, and upgrade support | High |

The value of a quest run depends on how many problems it solves at once. If a quest gives Gold but also finishes the ore you need for a tool upgrade, it is better than a pure payout objective. That is why material overlap matters so much in Subterra quests.
